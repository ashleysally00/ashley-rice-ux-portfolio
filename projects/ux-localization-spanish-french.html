UX Localization: Spanish & French

Testing multilingual UX language clarity across Spanish and French interfaces

Role

UX Writer
Localization Lead

Languages

Spanish (es-MX)
French (fr-FR)

Focus

Terminology
Comprehension

The Challenge

Direct translations of interface copy often fail to preserve meaning, tone, or usability. Common UX terms can vary significantly by language, region, and cultural context.

The challenge was to identify where translated UI terms introduced confusion or friction for users.

What I Did

  • Designed an interactive quiz to test understanding of common UX terms.
  • Created Spanish (es-MX) and French (fr-FR) versions with parallel structure.
  • Compared literal translations against more natural, user-friendly phrasing.
  • Observed where users hesitated, misinterpreted, or selected incorrect options.

Outcome

The experiment revealed clear gaps between technically correct translations and terms that users actually understand in context.

Results highlighted the importance of localization over direct translation in multilingual UX writing.
